Founded by Erno Goldfinger.

The story of Goldfinger furniture begins with the visionary architect and designer, Erno Goldfinger. Born in Hungary in 1902, Goldfinger was a pioneer of modernism, a movement that sought to break away from traditional design conventions and embrace simplicity, functionality, and clean lines. After moving to England in the 1930s, Goldfinger quickly gained recognition for his innovative approach to architecture and furniture design.

Goldfinger's furniture designs were characterized by their geometric shapes, clean lines, and the use of natural materials such as wood, leather, and metal. He believed that furniture should not only be functional but also aesthetically pleasing, reflecting the beauty and simplicity of the natural world. Goldfinger's designs were a stark contrast to the ornate and elaborate furniture styles that were popular at the time.

In 1946, Goldfinger co-founded the furniture company "Goldfinger Designs" with his wife, Ursula Blackwell. The company produced a range of furniture pieces, including chairs, tables, sofas, and storage units. Goldfinger's designs quickly gained popularity among architects, designers, and art collectors, who were drawn to their timeless aesthetic and exceptional craftsmanship.

Goldfinger's legacy continues to inspire contemporary furniture designers, and his pieces are still highly sought after by collectors and design enthusiasts worldwide. His furniture is represented in major museums and galleries around the world, and his influence can be seen in the work of many modern designers.

Erno Goldfinger's pioneering spirit and innovative approach to design laid the foundation for the Goldfinger furniture brand, which has become synonymous with luxury, elegance, and sophistication.

Tips

Here are some practical tips to help you choose and care for your garden furniture:

Tip 1: Choose the right material for your needs
Consider the climate in your area, how often you will be using your garden furniture, and how much maintenance you are willing to do. If you live in a rainy climate, you may want to choose a material that is resistant to moisture, such as metal or plastic. If you have a busy lifestyle, you may want to choose a material that is low-maintenance, such as aluminum or teak.

Tip 2: Measure your space before you buy
Make sure you measure the space where you want to put your garden furniture before you buy it. This will help you avoid buying pieces that are too big or too small for your space. It is also important to consider the layout of your garden and how you want to use the space. For example, if you want to create a dining area, you will need to make sure you have enough space for a table and chairs.

Tip 3: Choose comfortable furniture
You will be spending a lot of time in your garden furniture, so it is important to choose pieces that are comfortable. Look for furniture with cushions that are firm enough to provide support, but soft enough to be comfortable. You may also want to consider choosing furniture with adjustable backs or armrests.

Tip 4: Protect your garden furniture from the elements
If you live in a climate with extreme weather conditions, you will need to take steps to protect your garden furniture from the elements. This may include covering it with a tarp or storing it in a shed or garage during the off-season. You should also clean your garden furniture regularly to remove dirt and debris.

By following these tips, you can choose and care for garden furniture that will provide you with years of enjoyment.
