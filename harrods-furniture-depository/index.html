Established in 1849

The Harrods story began in 1849 when Charles Henry Harrod, a young man with a passion for entrepreneurship, opened a small shop on London's Brompton Road.

  • Humble Beginnings:

    Harrod's initial venture was a modest grocery store, selling tea, coffee, and other basic provisions. However, his keen eye for quality and customer service quickly set him apart from the competition.

  • Expansion and Diversification:

    As Harrods' reputation grew, so did its offerings. The store expanded, adding departments for clothing, furniture, and homewares. Harrods became known for its wide selection of high-quality goods, attracting discerning customers from across London and beyond.

  • A Destination for Luxury:

    By the turn of the 20th century, Harrods had firmly established itself as a premier destination for luxury shopping. Its opulent store on Brompton Road, with its grand architecture and elegant interiors, became a symbol of sophistication and refinement.

  • Furniture Depository Opens:

    In 1905, Harrods opened its dedicated Furniture Depository, just a short distance from the main store. This purpose-built facility allowed Harrods to showcase its extensive collection of furniture and furnishings in a spacious and inspiring setting.

Today, the Harrods Furniture Depository remains a cornerstone of the Harrods brand, offering an unparalleled selection of furniture, homeware, and interior design services. Its rich history and unwavering commitment to quality make it a beloved destination for discerning shoppers worldwide.

FAQ

Have questions about garden furniture? Here are some frequently asked questions to help you make informed decisions and create a stylish and functional outdoor space:

Question 1: What materials are best for garden furniture?
Answer 1: The best materials for garden furniture depend on your lifestyle, budget, and personal preferences. Popular options include teak, rattan, aluminum, and plastic. Teak is durable and weather-resistant, but also more expensive. Rattan is lightweight and comfortable, but requires regular maintenance. Aluminum is strong and rust-resistant, making it a good choice for coastal areas. Plastic is affordable and easy to clean, but may not be as durable as other materials.

Question 2: How can I choose garden furniture that is both stylish and comfortable?
Answer 2: When selecting garden furniture, consider both style and comfort. Choose pieces that complement your outdoor décor and personal taste. Look for furniture with comfortable cushions and ergonomic designs. Test the furniture before purchasing to ensure that it provides adequate support and relaxation.

Question 3: What are some space-saving garden furniture ideas for small spaces?
Answer 3: Maximize space in a small garden with compact and versatile furniture. Consider foldable or stackable chairs and tables that can be easily stored when not in use. Benches with built-in storage are also a great way to save space and keep cushions and other items organized.

Question 4: How can I protect my garden furniture from the elements?
Answer 4: To protect your garden furniture from rain, sun damage, and other harsh weather conditions, use protective covers or store the furniture indoors during inclement weather. Apply teak oil or other appropriate treatments to wooden furniture to maintain its appearance and longevity.

Question 5: What are some easy ways to add a personal touch to my garden furniture?
Answer 5: Personalize your garden furniture with colorful cushions, throws, and outdoor rugs. Add potted plants, flowers, and other decorative elements to create a cozy and inviting atmosphere. String lights and lanterns can also enhance the ambiance, especially during evening gatherings.

Question 6: Where can I find high-quality garden furniture?
Answer 6: For a wide selection of high-quality garden furniture, visit reputable furniture stores, home improvement centers, and online retailers. Consider shopping at specialty stores that focus on outdoor furniture to find unique and stylish pieces. Read reviews and compare prices to ensure you're getting the best value for your money.

With careful consideration and a bit of creativity, you can choose garden furniture that not only looks great but also provides comfort and functionality for your outdoor space.

Tips

Here are some practical tips to help you choose, arrange, and maintain your garden furniture for a stylish and inviting outdoor space:

Tip 1: Plan Your Layout:
Before purchasing furniture, take some time to plan the layout of your garden. Consider the size and shape of the space, as well as the activities you plan to do outdoors. Arrange furniture in a way that promotes conversation and allows for easy movement. Leave enough space for walkways and access to other areas of the garden.

Tip 2: Mix and Match:
Don't be afraid to mix and match different styles and materials of garden furniture. This can create a more eclectic and personal look. For example, you could pair a modern teak dining table with rustic rattan chairs. Or, add a few colorful plastic Adirondack chairs to a traditional wooden patio set.

Tip 3: Add Comfort and Style with Cushions and Accessories:
Make your garden furniture more comfortable and stylish with cushions, throws, and other accessories. Choose cushions that are weather-resistant and easy to clean. Add outdoor rugs, lanterns, and potted plants to create a cozy and inviting atmosphere.

Tip 4: Maintain Your Garden Furniture:
To keep your garden furniture looking its best and lasting for years to come, regular maintenance is essential. Clean the furniture regularly with mild soap and water. Apply teak oil or other appropriate treatments to wooden furniture to protect it from the elements. Store cushions and other accessories indoors when not in use to prevent fading and damage.

By following these tips, you can create a beautiful and functional garden space that you and your loved ones can enjoy for many years to come.

With careful planning, a bit of creativity, and regular maintenance, your garden furniture can transform your outdoor space into an oasis of comfort, style, and relaxation.
