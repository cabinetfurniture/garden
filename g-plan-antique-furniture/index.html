Founded in 1952 by Eric Gomme.

In the midst of post-war Britain, a visionary furniture maker named Eric Gomme embarked on a transformative journey that would forever shape the world of furniture design. In 1952, he established G Plan, a company destined to become synonymous with timeless elegance and unwavering quality.

Driven by a passion for innovation and a deep understanding of the evolving needs of modern living, Gomme sought to create furniture that was both stylish and functional. He recognized the potential of mass production to make well-designed furniture accessible to a wider audience, while maintaining the highest standards of craftsmanship.

Gomme's unwavering commitment to quality and attention to detail quickly gained recognition, propelling G Plan to the forefront of the furniture industry. The company's iconic designs, characterized by their clean lines, minimalist aesthetic, and expert use of materials, soon became the epitome of mid-century modern style.

Under Gomme's leadership, G Plan collaborated with renowned designers such as Ib Kofod-Larsen, Donald Gomme, and Victor Wilkins, who played instrumental roles in shaping the company's distinctive aesthetic. Their innovative designs, combined with Gomme's business acumen, catapulted G Plan to international acclaim, making it one of the most influential furniture brands of the 20th century.

Eric Gomme's legacy continues to inspire and influence furniture design to this day. His vision of creating accessible, stylish, and functional furniture has left an indelible mark on the industry, and G Plan antique furniture remains highly sought after by collectors and design enthusiasts worldwide.

Revolutionized furniture industry.

G Plan's impact on the furniture industry was nothing short of revolutionary. Eric Gomme's innovative approach to furniture design and production challenged conventional norms and set new standards for the industry.

G Plan was among the first companies to embrace mass production techniques, recognizing their potential to make well-designed furniture more accessible to the general public. However, Gomme refused to compromise on quality, insisting on using only the finest materials and employing skilled craftsmen to ensure that each piece met his exacting standards.

G Plan's designs were a breath of fresh air in a market dominated by traditional, ornate styles. The company's focus on clean lines, minimalist aesthetics, and functional forms appealed to a new generation of consumers who sought modern, stylish furniture for their homes.

Gomme's visionary leadership and the company's commitment to innovation and quality propelled G Plan to the forefront of the furniture industry. Its iconic designs, such as the Fresco, C Range, and Tola and G-Plan ranges, became symbols of mid-century modern style and continue to be highly sought after by collectors and design enthusiasts today.

G Plan's revolutionary approach to furniture design and production left a lasting legacy on the industry. The company's emphasis on quality, functionality, and accessibility helped democratize good design, making it available to a wider audience. G Plan's influence can still be seen in contemporary furniture design, a testament to its enduring impact on the industry.

Blend of innovation and functionality.

G Plan's furniture was renowned for its seamless blend of innovation and functionality, a hallmark that set it apart from its contemporaries.

  • Clean lines and minimalist aesthetic:

    G Plan's designs were characterized by their clean lines, simple forms, and minimalist aesthetic. This focus on pared-down elegance not only reflected the mid-century modern style but also ensured that the furniture would remain timeless and relevant for years to come.

  • Multifunctional furniture:

    G Plan recognized the space constraints faced by many homeowners, especially in urban areas. In response, the company introduced multifunctional furniture that could serve multiple purposes. For example, the popular Tola and G-Plan ranges featured sofa beds, convertible desks, and storage units that maximized space utilization.

  • Adjustable and modular designs:

    G Plan also embraced adjustable and modular designs, allowing customers to customize their furniture to suit their specific needs and preferences. This flexibility made G Plan furniture highly adaptable to different living spaces and lifestyles.

  • High-quality materials and construction:

    Despite its focus on innovation and functionality, G Plan never compromised on quality. The company used only the finest materials, such as teak, walnut, and rosewood, and employed skilled craftsmen to ensure that each piece was meticulously constructed. This commitment to quality ensured that G Plan furniture was both durable and aesthetically pleasing.

G Plan's blend of innovation and functionality made its furniture not only stylish but also practical and adaptable to the needs of modern living. This combination of form and function is one of the key reasons why G Plan antique furniture remains so highly sought after today.

Pioneering designers: Kofod-Larsen, Gomme, Wilkins.

G Plan's success was greatly influenced by the contributions of its pioneering designers, who played a pivotal role in shaping the company's distinctive aesthetic and innovative approach to furniture design.

Ib Kofod-Larsen:
A Danish designer known for his minimalist style and focus on functionality, Kofod-Larsen joined G Plan in 1951. His designs, such as the iconic Fresco range, exemplified the Scandinavian design principles of simplicity, clean lines, and functionality. Kofod-Larsen's work helped establish G Plan's reputation for creating furniture that was both stylish and practical.

Donald Gomme:
The son of G Plan founder Eric Gomme, Donald Gomme was also a talented designer in his own right. He joined the company in the 1950s and quickly made a name for himself with his innovative and forward-thinking designs. Gomme's work often featured bold geometric shapes and striking colors, pushing the boundaries of mid-century modern design. His most famous design for G Plan is the C Range, a modular storage system that remains popular to this day.

Victor Wilkins:
Victor Wilkins joined G Plan in the 1960s and quickly became one of the company's most influential designers. His designs were known for their elegant simplicity and understated sophistication. Wilkins often drew inspiration from traditional British furniture forms, reimagining them with a modern twist. His work for G Plan includes the popular Tola and G-Plan ranges, which are characterized by their clean lines, sculptural forms, and rich woodgrains.

These pioneering designers, along with others, played a crucial role in establishing G Plan's reputation as a leading innovator in mid-century modern furniture design. Their collective vision and creativity helped shape the company's unique aesthetic, which continues to inspire furniture designers and collectors to this day.

Clean lines, minimalist aesthetic.

G Plan furniture was renowned for its clean lines and minimalist aesthetic, which epitomized the mid-century modern style. This design philosophy emphasized simplicity, functionality, and the absence of unnecessary ornamentation.

Simplicity of form:
G Plan designers favored simple, geometric forms over elaborate embellishments. They believed that the beauty of furniture lies in its essential shape and proportion, rather than in excessive decoration. Functionality and purpose:
G Plan furniture was designed with a strong emphasis on functionality and purpose. Every piece was carefully considered to ensure that it served its intended function efficiently and effectively. Absence of ornamentation:

Instead, they focused on creating furniture with clean, uncluttered lines that allowed the natural beauty of the materials to shine through.

G Plan's clean lines and minimalist aesthetic have stood the test of time, making its furniture as relevant and desirable today as it was when it was first produced. The simplicity and functionality of G Plan furniture make it a perfect choice for contemporary living spaces, where clean lines and uncluttered design are highly valued.

Use of teak, walnut, rosewood.

G Plan furniture was crafted using a variety of high-quality woods, including teak, walnut, and rosewood. These woods were selected for their exceptional beauty, durability, and ability to complement the company's clean and minimalist aesthetic.

Teak:
Teak was one of the most popular woods used by G Plan. Known for its rich golden brown color and natural resistance to rot and decay, teak was ideal for furniture that was intended to last. G Plan's teak furniture often featured bold, geometric shapes and simple, understated details, showcasing the natural beauty of the wood.

Walnut:
Walnut was another favored wood for G Plan furniture. Its deep, warm brown color and distinctive grain patterns added a touch of luxury and sophistication to any room. G Plan's walnut furniture often featured more intricate designs and elaborate details, highlighting the wood's inherent beauty and craftsmanship.

Rosewood:
Rosewood was used sparingly by G Plan, but it was highly prized for its unique reddish-brown color and exotic grain patterns. G Plan's rosewood furniture was often reserved for their most exclusive and high-end pieces, such as dining tables, credenzas, and bedroom sets. The use of rosewood added a touch of glamour and opulence to any space.

G Plan's use of teak, walnut, and rosewood not only ensured the durability and longevity of its furniture but also elevated its aesthetic appeal. The natural beauty of these woods complemented the company's clean lines and minimalist design, creating furniture that was both stylish and timeless.

FAQ

If you're considering adding garden furniture to your outdoor space, you may have a few questions. Here are some frequently asked questions and answers to help you make an informed decision:

Question 1: What are the different types of garden furniture?
Answer 1: Garden furniture comes in a wide variety of styles, materials, and designs. Common types include lounge chairs, dining sets, benches, swings, and sun loungers. You can choose pieces that best suit your needs and preferences.

Question 2: What materials are used for garden furniture?
Answer 2: Garden furniture is typically made from materials that can withstand outdoor conditions, such as teak, acacia, metal, plastic, and wicker. Teak and acacia are popular choices for their durability and natural resistance to rot and decay.

Question 3: How do I choose the right garden furniture for my space?
Answer 3: Consider the size of your outdoor space, the purpose of the furniture (e.g., dining, lounging, or entertaining), and your personal style. Choose pieces that are proportional to the space and that complement your existing décor.

Question 4: How do I care for my garden furniture?
Answer 4: Regular cleaning and maintenance will help prolong the life of your garden furniture. Clean the furniture with mild soap and water, and use a sealant or protector to protect it from the elements. Store cushions and other soft furnishings indoors when not in use.

Question 5: Can I leave my garden furniture outside all year round?
Answer 5: It depends on the material and construction of your furniture. Some materials, such as teak and metal, can withstand outdoor conditions year-round. However, it's generally recommended to store cushions and other soft furnishings indoors during inclement weather.

Question 6: Where can I buy garden furniture?
Answer 6: Garden furniture is available at a variety of retailers, both online and in-store. You can find a wide selection of styles, materials, and designs to suit your needs and budget.

Question 7: How can I make my garden furniture more comfortable?
Answer 7: Add cushions, pillows, and throws to your garden furniture to make it more comfortable and inviting. You can also use outdoor rugs and fairy lights to create a cozy and stylish ambiance.
