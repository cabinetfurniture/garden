Staining Wooden Garden Furniture A Step by Step Guide for a Beautiful Finish

Written by Eveline Mar 04, 2025 Â· 11 min read

If you're looking to give your wooden garden furniture a new lease on life, staining is a great option. Staining can help to protect the wood from the elements, while also adding a touch of color and style.

In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to stain wooden garden furniture. We'll cover everything from choosing the right stain to applying it correctly, so you can achieve a beautiful finish that will last for years to come.

Before you begin staining your garden furniture, it's important to clean it thoroughly. This will remove any dirt, grease, or other contaminants that could interfere with the staining process.

Clean thoroughly

The first step to staining wooden garden furniture is to clean it thoroughly. This will remove any dirt, grease, or other contaminants that could interfere with the staining process.

  • Use a mild detergent and water.

    Mix a small amount of mild detergent with water in a bucket. Use a soft cloth or sponge to apply the soapy water to the furniture, working in small sections. Rinse the furniture thoroughly with clean water and allow it to dry completely.

  • Remove any mildew or mold.

    If your furniture has any mildew or mold, you'll need to remove it before staining. You can do this by using a commercial mildew remover or by making your own solution with one part bleach and four parts water. Apply the solution to the affected areas and let it sit for 15 minutes. Rinse the furniture thoroughly with clean water and allow it to dry completely.

  • Sand the furniture.

    Once the furniture is clean and dry, you'll need to sand it lightly to smooth out any rough spots. Use a fine-grit sandpaper and sand in the direction of the grain. Be sure to remove all dust from the furniture before staining.

  • Protect metal parts.

    If your furniture has any metal parts, be sure to protect them from the stain. You can do this by covering them with masking tape or by applying a coat of clear sealant.

By following these steps, you can ensure that your furniture is clean and ready to be stained.

Choose right stain

Once you've cleaned and prepared your furniture, it's time to choose the right stain. There are many different types of stains available, so it's important to do your research and choose one that is right for your project.

Here are a few things to consider when choosing a stain:

  • The type of wood.
    Different types of wood absorb stain differently. Be sure to choose a stain that is specifically designed for the type of wood you're using.
  • The desired color.
    Stains come in a wide variety of colors, so you can choose one that matches your personal style and the overall look of your garden. You can also mix different stains to create custom colors.
  • The finish.
    Stains can be either transparent or opaque. Transparent stains allow the natural grain of the wood to show through, while opaque stains create a solid color. Choose the type of finish that you prefer.
  • The durability.
    Some stains are more durable than others. If you're looking for a stain that will last for many years, be sure to choose one that is specifically designed for outdoor use.

Once you've considered all of these factors, you can start narrowing down your choices. You may want to purchase a few sample cans of stain and test them on a small area of your furniture before making a final decision.

By taking the time to choose the right stain, you can achieve a beautiful, long-lasting finish on your wooden garden furniture.

Prep wood surface

Once you've chosen the right stain, it's time to prepare the wood surface. This will help the stain to penetrate the wood evenly and create a beautiful finish.

Here are a few steps to follow when prepping the wood surface:

  • Remove any old finish.
    If your furniture has been previously stained or painted, you'll need to remove the old finish before applying new stain. You can do this by using a chemical stripper or by sanding the furniture down to bare wood.
  • Sand the wood.
    Once the old finish has been removed, you'll need to sand the wood to smooth out any rough spots. Use a fine-grit sandpaper and sand in the direction of the grain. Be sure to remove all dust from the furniture before staining.
  • Clean the wood.
    After sanding, you'll need to clean the wood to remove any dust or debris. Use a tack cloth or a damp cloth to wipe down the furniture. Be sure to let the wood dry completely before staining.
  • Apply a pre-stain conditioner.
    A pre-stain conditioner can help the stain to penetrate the wood more evenly and create a more uniform finish. Apply the conditioner according to the manufacturer's instructions.

By following these steps, you can ensure that the wood surface is properly prepared for staining.

Tip: If you're staining new wood, you may need to raise the grain before staining. This can be done by wetting the wood with a damp cloth and then sanding it lightly with a fine-grit sandpaper. Be sure to remove all dust from the furniture before staining.

Apply stain evenly

Once the wood surface is prepared, it's time to apply the stain. Here are a few tips for applying stain evenly:

  • Use a high-quality stain brush.
    A good quality stain brush will help you to apply the stain evenly and smoothly. Choose a brush that is the right size for your project. A 2-inch brush is a good all-purpose size.
  • Apply the stain in thin coats.
    Don't try to apply the stain too thickly in one coat. Apply it in thin, even coats, allowing each coat to dry completely before applying the next. This will help to prevent the stain from becoming blotchy or uneven.
  • Work in small sections.
    Don't try to stain the entire piece of furniture at once. Work in small sections, such as one leg or one side of a table. This will help you to apply the stain more evenly and avoid missing any spots.
  • Follow the grain of the wood.
    When applying the stain, follow the grain of the wood. This will help the stain to penetrate the wood more evenly and create a more natural finish.
  • Wipe off excess stain.
    After applying the stain, wipe off any excess stain with a clean cloth. This will help to prevent the stain from becoming too dark or blotchy.

By following these tips, you can apply the stain evenly and achieve a beautiful, professional-looking finish.

Tip: If you're staining a large piece of furniture, you may want to use a sprayer to apply the stain. This can help you to apply the stain more evenly and quickly.

Protect with sealant

Once the stain has dried completely, you'll need to apply a sealant to protect the finish. A sealant will help to protect the wood from the elements and prevent the stain from fading or peeling.

  • Choose the right sealant.

    There are many different types of sealants available, so it's important to choose one that is right for your project. For outdoor furniture, you'll want to choose a sealant that is specifically designed for outdoor use. You can choose between water-based sealants, oil-based sealants, and penetrating sealants.

  • Apply the sealant according to the manufacturer's instructions.

    Each type of sealant has its own application instructions. Be sure to read and follow the instructions carefully to ensure that you apply the sealant correctly.

  • Apply multiple coats of sealant.

    For best results, apply multiple coats of sealant. This will help to create a strong, durable finish that will protect your furniture for years to come.

  • Reapply sealant periodically.

    Even the best sealants will eventually need to be reapplied. Be sure to inspect your furniture regularly and reapply sealant as needed.

By following these tips, you can protect your stained wooden garden furniture from the elements and keep it looking its best for years to come.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about staining garden furniture:

Question 1: What is the best type of stain for garden furniture?
Answer 1: The best type of stain for garden furniture is one that is specifically designed for outdoor use. These stains are typically more durable and resistant to fading and peeling than indoor stains.

Question 2: How do I prepare my garden furniture for staining?
Answer 2: To prepare your garden furniture for staining, you should first clean it thoroughly to remove any dirt, grease, or other contaminants. Next, sand the furniture to smooth out any rough spots. Finally, apply a pre-stain conditioner to help the stain penetrate the wood more evenly.

Question 3: How do I apply stain to my garden furniture?
Answer 3: To apply stain to your garden furniture, use a high-quality stain brush. Apply the stain in thin, even coats, working in small sections. Follow the grain of the wood and wipe off any excess stain with a clean cloth.

Question 4: How long does it take for stain to dry?
Answer 4: The drying time for stain varies depending on the type of stain and the weather conditions. In general, you should allow at least 24 hours for the stain to dry completely before applying a sealant.

Question 5: What is the best way to protect my stained garden furniture?
Answer 5: The best way to protect your stained garden furniture is to apply a sealant. Sealants help to protect the wood from the elements and prevent the stain from fading or peeling. You should apply multiple coats of sealant and reapply it periodically to keep your furniture looking its best.

Question 6: Can I stain my garden furniture if it is already painted?
Answer 6: Yes, you can stain your garden furniture if it is already painted. However, you will need to remove the old paint first. You can do this by using a chemical stripper or by sanding the furniture down to bare wood.

Question 7: How often should I stain my garden furniture?
Answer 7: You should stain your garden furniture every 1-2 years, or more often if it is exposed to harsh weather conditions.
