  • Polyester finish: This type of finish is slightly less reflective than a piano finish, but it is also more durable. It is a good choice for furniture that will be used frequently.
  • Lacquer finish: This type of finish is similar to a polyester finish, but it is slightly more durable. It is a good choice for furniture that will be exposed to a lot of wear and tear.
  • Veneer finish: This type of finish is made by applying a thin layer of high gloss material to a substrate, such as wood or MDF. It is a good choice for furniture that you want to have a high gloss finish, but you don't want to pay the price for a solid high gloss piece.

Clean regularly: High gloss furniture requires regular cleaning to keep it looking its best.

High gloss furniture is easy to clean, but it does require regular cleaning to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips for cleaning high gloss furniture:

  • Use a soft, clean cloth. Do not use abrasive cloths or sponges, as these can scratch the finish.
  • Dampen the cloth with water. Do not use cleaning solutions, as these can damage the finish.
  • Wipe the furniture in the direction of the grain. This will help to prevent scratches.
  • Buff the furniture with a dry cloth. This will help to restore the shine to the finish.

By following these tips, you can keep your high gloss furniture looking its best for years to come.
