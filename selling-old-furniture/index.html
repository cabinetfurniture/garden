Turn Your Old Furniture into Cash A Comprehensive Guide to Selling Used Furniture

Written by Jack Mar 16, 2025 Â· 13 min read

Are you downsizing, decluttering, or simply looking to refresh your living space? Selling your old furniture is an excellent way to make some extra cash and give your pre-loved pieces a new home. Whether you're a seasoned seller or just getting started, this guide will provide you with all the information you need to successfully sell your used furniture.

From preparing your furniture for sale to determining the right price and choosing the best platform, we've got you covered. We'll also share tips on how to haggle with potential buyers and close the deal with confidence. So, if you're ready to turn your old furniture into cash, let's dive right in.

Before you start selling your furniture, it's important to take the time to prepare it properly. This will help you get the best price and make the process smoother for potential buyers.

Selling Old Furniture

To make the process of selling your used furniture as smooth and successful as possible, keep these eight important points in mind:

  • Declutter and Depersonalize
  • Clean and Repair
  • Take High-Quality Photos
  • Write Detailed Descriptions
  • Price Strategically
  • Choose the Right Platform
  • Be Prepared to Negotiate
  • Close the Deal Safely

By following these tips, you can increase your chances of selling your old furniture quickly and for a fair price.

Declutter and Depersonalize

Before you take any photos or list your furniture for sale, it's important to declutter and depersonalize it. This means removing any personal items, such as photos, magazines, or other clutter, that could distract potential buyers from seeing the furniture itself.

  • Remove all personal items: This includes anything that could identify you or your family, such as photos, artwork, or mail.

    Potential buyers want to be able to imagine themselves living with the furniture, so it's important to make it as neutral as possible.

  • Declutter and clean the space: This means removing any unnecessary items from the room and cleaning up any messes. A cluttered or dirty space will make your furniture look less appealing.
  • Depersonalize the furniture: This means removing any unique or personal touches that you've added to the furniture. For example, if you have a couch with a slipcover, remove the slipcover so that potential buyers can see the original fabric.
  • Stage the furniture: Once you've decluttered and depersonalized the furniture, you can stage it to make it look more appealing to potential buyers. This means arranging the furniture in a way that shows off its best features and makes it look like a part of a cohesive living space.

Decluttering and depersonalizing your furniture will help you get the best price and make it more appealing to potential buyers. By following these tips, you can increase your chances of selling your old furniture quickly and easily.

Clean and Repair

Once you've decluttered and depersonalized your furniture, it's time to clean and repair it. This will make your furniture look its best and increase its value.

Start by cleaning your furniture thoroughly with a mild soap and water solution. Be sure to get into all the nooks and crannies, and don't forget to clean the upholstery. If your furniture is made of wood, you can use a furniture polish to help protect it and make it shine.

Once your furniture is clean, inspect it for any damage. If you find any scratches or dents, you can try to repair them yourself using a furniture repair kit. If the damage is more severe, you may need to hire a professional to repair it.

By cleaning and repairing your furniture, you can make it look its best and increase its value. This will help you get the best price when you sell it.

Here are some additional tips for cleaning and repairing your furniture:

  • Use the right cleaning products: Different types of furniture require different cleaning products. For example, you should use a mild soap and water solution to clean wood furniture, but you can use a stronger cleaner to clean metal furniture.
  • Test cleaning products in an inconspicuous area: Before you apply any cleaning product to your furniture, test it in an inconspicuous area to make sure it doesn't damage the finish.
  • Be gentle when cleaning: Don't scrub your furniture too hard, as this could damage the finish.
  • Repair damage promptly: If you find any damage to your furniture, repair it promptly to prevent it from getting worse.

Take High-Quality Photos

High-quality photos are essential for selling your used furniture online. Potential buyers want to be able to see the furniture clearly from all angles, so it's important to take photos that are well-lit and in focus.

  • Use natural light: If possible, take photos of your furniture in natural light. This will help to show the true colors and textures of the furniture.
  • Take photos from different angles: Take photos of your furniture from different angles so that potential buyers can get a good sense of its size and shape.
  • Use a high-quality camera or smartphone: The better the quality of your camera, the better your photos will be. If you don't have a high-quality camera, you can use your smartphone to take photos, but be sure to use the highest quality setting.
  • Edit your photos: Once you've taken your photos, you can edit them to improve their quality. You can use a photo editing program to crop the photos, adjust the brightness and contrast, and remove any unwanted objects.

By taking high-quality photos, you can make your furniture look its best and attract more potential buyers.

Write Detailed Descriptions

In addition to taking high-quality photos, it's also important to write detailed descriptions of your furniture. This will help potential buyers to understand the condition of the furniture, its dimensions, and any other relevant information.

  • Be honest and accurate: When writing your descriptions, be honest and accurate about the condition of the furniture. Don't try to hide any flaws, as this will only disappoint potential buyers.
  • Include all relevant information: In your descriptions, be sure to include all relevant information about the furniture, such as its dimensions, materials, and age.
  • Use descriptive language: Use descriptive language to help potential buyers visualize the furniture. For example, instead of saying "couch," you could say "comfortable and stylish couch with soft cushions and a sturdy frame."
  • Proofread your descriptions: Before you publish your listings, be sure to proofread your descriptions for any errors.

By writing detailed descriptions, you can help potential buyers to make informed decisions about whether or not to purchase your furniture.

Price Strategically

Pricing your furniture strategically is important for getting the best possible price. If you price your furniture too high, potential buyers will be scared away. If you price it too low, you'll end up losing money.

  • Research comparable prices: Before you price your furniture, take some time to research comparable prices. You can do this by looking at online marketplaces, such as eBay and Craigslist, or by visiting local furniture stores.
  • Consider the condition of your furniture: When pricing your furniture, be sure to consider its condition. Furniture that is in good condition can be priced higher than furniture that is in poor condition.
  • Set a realistic price: When setting a price for your furniture, be realistic about what you can expect to get for it. Don't overprice your furniture, as this will only deter potential buyers.
  • Be prepared to negotiate: Be prepared to negotiate with potential buyers. However, don't be afraid to walk away from a deal if you're not getting a fair price for your furniture.

By pricing your furniture strategically, you can increase your chances of getting the best possible price.

Choose the Right Platform

There are a number of different platforms that you can use to sell your used furniture. The best platform for you will depend on the type of furniture you're selling, your target audience, and your budget.

  • Online marketplaces: Online marketplaces, such as eBay and Craigslist, are a great option for selling used furniture. These platforms have a large audience of potential buyers, and they're relatively easy to use.
  • Social media: You can also sell your used furniture on social media platforms, such as Facebook Marketplace and Instagram. These platforms are a great way to reach a local audience, and they're free to use.
  • Consignment stores: Consignment stores are a good option if you don't want to deal with the hassle of selling your furniture yourself. These stores will sell your furniture for you on a commission basis.
  • Local classifieds: Local classifieds, such as newspapers and online classifieds websites, are a good option for selling used furniture locally.

Once you've chosen a platform, be sure to create a listing that is clear, concise, and informative. Include high-quality photos of your furniture, and be sure to price it strategically.

Be Prepared to Negotiate

When selling your used furniture, be prepared to negotiate with potential buyers. This doesn't mean that you have to give your furniture away for free, but it does mean that you should be willing to compromise on the price.

Here are a few tips for negotiating with potential buyers:

  • Be realistic about your price: When setting a price for your furniture, be realistic about what you can expect to get for it. Don't overprice your furniture, as this will only deter potential buyers.
  • Be willing to compromise: When negotiating with potential buyers, be willing to compromise on the price. However, don't be afraid to walk away from a deal if you're not getting a fair price for your furniture.
  • Be prepared to walk away: If you're not comfortable with the price that a potential buyer is offering, be prepared to walk away from the deal. There are plenty of other potential buyers out there.

By being prepared to negotiate, you can increase your chances of getting a fair price for your used furniture.

Here are some additional tips for negotiating with potential buyers:

  • Be polite and respectful: Even if you're not able to come to an agreement with a potential buyer, be polite and respectful. You never know when you might cross paths with them again.
  • Be firm but fair: When negotiating with potential buyers, be firm but fair. Don't be afraid to stand up for what you believe is a fair price for your furniture.
  • Be prepared to compromise: As mentioned above, be prepared to compromise on the price. However, don't be afraid to walk away from a deal if you're not getting a fair price for your furniture.

Close the Deal Safely

Once you've found a buyer for your used furniture, it's important to close the deal safely. This means taking steps to protect yourself from fraud and ensuring that you get paid for your furniture.

Here are a few tips for closing the deal safely:

  • Meet in a public place: If possible, meet the buyer in a public place to exchange the furniture and payment. This will help to protect you from being robbed or scammed.
  • Inspect the payment: Before you hand over the furniture, inspect the payment to make sure that it is valid. If you're accepting a check, be sure to verify that the check is from a legitimate bank account.
  • Get a receipt: Once you've received payment, be sure to get a receipt from the buyer. This will serve as proof of the transaction.
  • Trust your instincts: If something about the buyer or the deal seems fishy, trust your instincts and walk away. It's better to be safe than sorry.

By following these tips, you can help to ensure that you close the deal safely and get paid for your used furniture.

Here are some additional tips for closing the deal safely:

  • Be aware of scams: There are a number of scams that can occur when selling used furniture. Be aware of these scams and take steps to protect yourself.
  • Never wire money: If a buyer asks you to wire them money, it is likely a scam. Never wire money to someone you don't know.
  • Use a payment service: If you're not comfortable accepting cash or a check, you can use a payment service, such as PayPal or Venmo.
  • Meet in a well-lit area: If you're meeting the buyer in a public place, be sure to meet in a well-lit area.

Here are some frequently asked questions about selling garden furniture:

Question: How can I clean my garden furniture?

Answer: The best way to clean your garden furniture depends on the material. For example, you can clean wooden furniture with a mixture of soap and water. You can clean metal furniture with a mild detergent and water. And you can clean plastic furniture with a mixture of vinegar and water.

Question: How can I protect my garden furniture from the elements?

Answer: You can protect your garden furniture from the elements by covering it with a tarp or furniture cover. You can also apply a sealant to the furniture to help protect it from moisture and UV rays.

Question: How can I make my garden furniture more comfortable?

Answer: You can make your garden furniture more comfortable by adding cushions and pillows. You can also add a throw blanket for extra warmth on chilly evenings.

Question: How can I sell my garden furniture?

Answer: You can sell your garden furniture online, through a consignment store, or at a garage sale. Be sure to clean and repair your furniture before selling it, and take high-quality photos to showcase its condition.

Question: How can I get the best price for my garden furniture?

Answer: To get the best price for your garden furniture, research comparable prices online and in local stores. Price your furniture strategically, and be prepared to negotiate with potential buyers.

Question: What should I do if my garden furniture is damaged?

Answer: If your garden furniture is damaged, you can try to repair it yourself using a furniture repair kit. If the damage is more severe, you may need to hire a professional to repair it.
