Easy to Maintain and Care For

Teakwood furniture is remarkably easy to maintain and care for. Its natural resistance to decay and insects means that it does not require regular treatment with chemical preservatives or sealants.

  • Regular cleaning: Teakwood furniture can be cleaned with a damp cloth and a mild soap solution. Avoid using harsh chemicals or abrasive cleaners, as these can damage the wood.
  • Occasional oiling: Teakwood furniture will naturally develop a silver-gray patina over time. If you prefer to maintain the original golden-brown color of the wood, you can apply teak oil every few months. Teak oil will help to restore the wood's natural oils and protect it from the elements.
  • Avoid direct sunlight: While teakwood is resistant to UV rays, it is best to avoid placing teakwood furniture in direct sunlight for extended periods of time. Direct sunlight can cause the wood to fade and become brittle.
  • Store indoors during winter: If you live in a climate with cold winters, it is best to store teakwood furniture indoors during the winter months. This will protect the wood from freezing and thawing, which can damage the wood.

By following these simple care and maintenance tips, you can keep your teakwood furniture looking its best for many years to come.
