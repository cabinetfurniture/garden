Design meets functionality.

In the world of strictly furniture, design and functionality go hand in hand. Furniture pieces are not merely decorative objects; they serve specific purposes and must be tailored to the needs and preferences of the user.

  • Form follows function:

    This principle, popularized by architect Louis Sullivan, emphasizes that the shape and design of a furniture piece should be primarily determined by its intended function. A chair, for example, should be designed to provide comfortable seating, while a table should be designed to provide a stable surface for dining or working.

  • Ergonomics in design:

    Ergonomics is the science of designing furniture and other products to fit the human body and promote comfort and productivity. Strictly furniture takes ergonomics into account, ensuring that furniture pieces are designed to minimize strain and discomfort, even during prolonged use.

  • Multifunctional furniture:

    In modern living spaces, where space is often at a premium, multifunctional furniture pieces are becoming increasingly popular. These pieces serve multiple purposes, saving space and adding versatility to a room. For example, a sofa bed can be used for both seating and sleeping, while a coffee table with built-in storage can be used to store books, magazines, and other items.

  • Personalization and customization:

Customization options allow customers to tailor furniture pieces to their specific requirements, ensuring that the furniture not only meets their functional needs but also reflects their personal style and taste.

By seamlessly blending design and functionality, strictly furniture creates pieces that are both aesthetically pleasing and practical, enhancing the overall experience and comfort of living spaces.

FAQ

Have questions about garden furniture? We've compiled a list of frequently asked questions to help you make informed decisions when choosing and caring for your outdoor furniture.

Question 1: What materials are best for garden furniture?
Answer 1: The best materials for garden furniture depend on your specific needs and preferences. Popular choices include teak, acacia, metal (such as aluminum or wrought iron), and wicker (natural or synthetic). Consider factors like durability, weather resistance, and maintenance requirements when making your decision.

Question 2: How can I protect my garden furniture from the elements?
Answer 2: To protect your garden furniture from the elements, you can:

Apply a sealant or water-resistant coating to wooden furniture. Use weather-resistant cushions and covers when not in use. Store furniture in a dry place during harsh weather conditions.

Question 3: What are some tips for maintaining garden furniture?
Answer 3: Regular maintenance can extend the lifespan of your garden furniture. Here are some tips:

Clean your furniture regularly with a mild detergent and water. Tighten screws and bolts periodically. Inspect furniture for any signs of damage and repair promptly.

Question 4: How can I create a comfortable and inviting outdoor space with garden furniture?
Answer 4: To create a comfortable and inviting outdoor space with garden furniture:

Choose furniture that is proportionate to the size of your space. Arrange furniture in a way that promotes conversation and relaxation. Add cushions, throws, and other accessories to enhance comfort and style.

Question 5: Can I leave my garden furniture outdoors all year round?
Answer 5: Whether you can leave your garden furniture outdoors all year round depends on the materials and climate. Some materials, such as teak and aluminum, are more resistant to weather damage than others. If you live in an area with harsh winters, it's best to store your furniture indoors or use weather-resistant covers.

Question 6: Where can I find high-quality garden furniture?
Answer 6: You can find high-quality garden furniture at reputable furniture stores, both online and offline. Look for stores that offer a wide selection of styles, materials, and brands. Read reviews and compare prices before making a purchase.

By following these tips and caring for your garden furniture properly, you can enjoy a beautiful and functional outdoor space for years to come.

Tips

Here are some practical tips to help you choose, arrange, and care for your garden furniture:

Tip 1: Choose the right materials:

Consider the climate in your area and the intended use of your furniture when choosing materials. For example, if you live in a rainy area, you may want to choose teak or aluminum furniture, which is naturally weather-resistant. If you have young children or pets, you may want to choose furniture made from durable materials that can withstand rough play.

Tip 2: Measure your space before you buy:

Make sure you know the dimensions of your outdoor space before you start shopping for furniture. This will help you choose pieces that are the right size and scale for your area. You don't want to end up with furniture that is too big or too small for your space.

Tip 3: Arrange your furniture for comfort and conversation:

When arranging your garden furniture, think about how you want to use the space. If you want to create a space for conversation, arrange your furniture in a circle or square so that everyone can face each other. If you want to create a space for relaxing, you may want to arrange your furniture in a more spread-out fashion.

Tip 4: Protect your furniture from the elements:

To extend the lifespan of your garden furniture, protect it from the elements. If you live in an area with harsh winters, store your furniture indoors or use weather-resistant covers. If you live in an area with a lot of sun, choose furniture that is UV-resistant to prevent fading.

By following these tips, you can choose, arrange, and care for your garden furniture to create a beautiful and inviting outdoor space that you can enjoy for years to come.

With careful consideration and proper care, your garden furniture can become a cherished part of your outdoor living space, providing a comfortable and stylish oasis for relaxation and enjoyment.
