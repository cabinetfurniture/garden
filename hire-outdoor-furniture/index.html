The Ultimate Guide to Hiring Outdoor Furniture

Written by Christine Apr 23, 2025 · 11 min read

Are you planning an outdoor party or event, but don't have the furniture to accommodate your guests? Hiring outdoor furniture is a great option for creating a comfortable and stylish space without breaking the bank. In this article, we'll provide you with everything you need to know about hiring outdoor furniture, from choosing the right pieces to setting up and taking down.

Whether you're hosting a small gathering or a large-scale event, hiring outdoor furniture can provide the perfect solution for your seating needs. With a wide range of furniture styles and rental options available, you're sure to find the perfect pieces to create the ambience you desire.

Now that you know the basics of hiring outdoor furniture, let's move on to the main content section of this article, where we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide to help you make the most of your rental experience.

hire outdoor furniture

When it comes to hiring outdoor furniture, there are a few key things to keep in mind:

  • Choose the right pieces
  • Consider your budget
  • Factor in delivery and setup
  • Read the rental agreement
  • Inspect the furniture before use
  • Use the furniture responsibly
  • Clean the furniture before returning it
  • Return the furniture on time

By following these tips, you can ensure that your outdoor furniture rental experience is a positive one.

Choose the right pieces

When choosing the right pieces of outdoor furniture to hire, there are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Size and shape: Consider the size and shape of your outdoor space. You'll want to choose pieces that are the right size for your space and that will allow your guests to move around comfortably.
  • Style: Choose pieces that match the style of your event. If you're having a casual party, you might want to choose more relaxed pieces. If you're having a more formal event, you might want to choose more elegant pieces.
  • Comfort: Make sure the pieces you choose are comfortable for your guests. You don't want your guests to be uncomfortable or in pain after sitting in your furniture for a few hours.
  • Durability: Choose pieces that are durable and can withstand the elements. If you're having your event outdoors, you'll want to choose pieces that are made from weather-resistant materials.

Once you've considered all of these factors, you can start to narrow down your choices. You can browse online rental catalogs or visit a local rental store to see what's available. Be sure to ask the rental company about the different pieces they have available and their recommendations for your event.

Consider your budget

When it comes to hiring outdoor furniture, it's important to consider your budget. Outdoor furniture rental prices can vary depending on the type of furniture, the quantity of furniture, and the length of the rental period. Here are a few tips for staying within your budget:

  • Set a budget before you start shopping: This will help you narrow down your choices and avoid overspending.
  • Compare prices from different rental companies: Be sure to get quotes from multiple companies before making a decision.
  • Consider renting used furniture: Used furniture is often available at a lower cost than new furniture.
  • Negotiate with the rental company: You may be able to get a discount if you rent furniture for a longer period of time or if you rent multiple pieces.

Once you've considered all of these factors, you can start to narrow down your choices and find the best deal on outdoor furniture rental.

Factor in delivery and setup

When hiring outdoor furniture, it's important to factor in the cost of delivery and setup. Delivery fees can vary depending on the distance from the rental company to your event location. Setup fees can also vary, depending on the complexity of the setup. Here are a few tips for saving money on delivery and setup:

Ask about free delivery: Some rental companies offer free delivery for orders over a certain amount. Be sure to ask about this when you're getting quotes from different companies.

Choose a rental company that's close to your event location: This will help to reduce the cost of delivery. You can also ask the rental company if they offer any discounts for local deliveries.

Set up the furniture yourself: If you're handy, you can save money by setting up the furniture yourself. Be sure to ask the rental company for instructions on how to set up the furniture properly.

Get help from friends or family: If you don't want to set up the furniture yourself, you can ask friends or family to help you. This is a great way to save money and get the furniture set up quickly and easily.

By following these tips, you can save money on delivery and setup when hiring outdoor furniture.

Read the rental agreement

Before you sign the rental agreement, it's important to read it carefully and make sure you understand all of the terms and conditions. The rental agreement should include information about the following:

  • The type and quantity of furniture you're renting: Make sure the agreement lists all of the pieces of furniture you're renting, as well as the quantity of each piece.
  • The rental period: The agreement should specify the start and end dates of your rental period.
  • The delivery and setup fees: The agreement should include the cost of delivery and setup, as well as any additional fees that may apply.
  • The damage deposit: The agreement may require you to pay a damage deposit. This deposit will be refunded to you at the end of your rental period, provided that the furniture is returned in good condition.

By reading the rental agreement carefully before you sign it, you can avoid any surprises later on.

Use the furniture responsibly

When using rented outdoor furniture, it's important to use it responsibly. This means taking care of the furniture and using it in a way that it was intended to be used. Here are a few tips for using rented outdoor furniture responsibly:

Don't overload the furniture: Avoid overloading the furniture with too many people or too much weight. This can damage the furniture and make it unsafe to use.

Don't use the furniture in a way that it wasn't intended to be used: For example, don't use a chair as a step stool or a table as a dance floor. This can damage the furniture and make it unsafe to use.

Keep the furniture clean: Wipe up spills and clean the furniture regularly to prevent stains and damage. You can use a mild soap and water solution to clean the furniture.

Store the furniture properly: When you're not using the furniture, store it in a dry, protected place. This will help to prevent the furniture from being damaged by the elements.

By following these tips, you can help to keep your rented outdoor furniture in good condition and avoid any additional charges.

Clean the furniture before returning it

Before you return the rented outdoor furniture, it's important to clean it. This will help to remove any dirt, debris, or stains that may have accumulated during your event. Here are a few tips for cleaning rented outdoor furniture:

  • Wipe down the furniture with a damp cloth: Use a mild soap and water solution to wipe down the furniture. Be sure to rinse the furniture thoroughly with clean water after cleaning it with soap and water.
  • Use a soft brush to remove any stubborn dirt or debris: If there is any stubborn dirt or debris on the furniture, use a soft brush to remove it. Be careful not to scratch the furniture with the brush.
  • Vacuum the furniture to remove any dust or pollen: If the furniture is upholstered, vacuum it to remove any dust or pollen that may have accumulated. Be sure to use the upholstery attachment on your vacuum cleaner.
  • Dry the furniture completely before storing it: Once you have cleaned the furniture, dry it completely with a clean towel. This will help to prevent the furniture from rusting or mildewing.

By following these tips, you can help to keep your rented outdoor furniture in good condition and avoid any additional charges.

Return the furniture on time

It's important to return the rented outdoor furniture on time. This will help to avoid any late fees and ensure that the furniture is available for other customers. Here are a few tips for returning rented outdoor furniture on time:

  • Check the rental agreement for the return date and time: The rental agreement should specify the date and time that the furniture is due back. Be sure to mark this date and time on your calendar.
  • Allow yourself plenty of time to return the furniture: Give yourself plenty of time to return the furniture, even if you think you'll be finished with it early. This will help to avoid any last-minute surprises.
  • Disassemble the furniture if necessary: If the furniture is large or bulky, you may need to disassemble it before returning it. Be sure to follow the instructions in the rental agreement for disassembling the furniture.
  • Return the furniture to the rental company: Return the furniture to the rental company on the date and time specified in the rental agreement. Be sure to bring the rental agreement with you when you return the furniture.

By following these tips, you can help to avoid any late fees and ensure that the furniture is available for other customers.
