Sell Secondhand Furniture for Extra Cash and Declutter Your Home

Written by Daniel Oct 29, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Have you got unwanted furniture cluttering up your home? Don't throw it away! You can sell secondhand furniture and make some extra cash while decluttering your space. Selling secondhand furniture is becoming increasingly popular as people look for sustainable and affordable ways to furnish their homes.

In this article, we'll guide you through the process of selling secondhand furniture. We'll cover everything from preparing your furniture for sale to listing it online or selling it to local stores. Whether you're selling a single item or an entire household of furniture, this article will provide you with the tips and advice you need to get the best possible price for your used goods.

Before we dive into the details of selling secondhand furniture, let's take a moment to consider the benefits of doing so.

Sell Secondhand Furniture

Selling secondhand furniture can be a great way to declutter your home, make some extra cash, and help the environment. Here are five important points to keep in mind when selling secondhand furniture:

  • Clean and repair: Make sure your furniture is clean and in good condition before selling it.
  • Price it right: Research similar items online to get an idea of what your furniture is worth.
  • Take good photos: Clear, well-lit photos will make your furniture more appealing to potential buyers.
  • Write a detailed description: Include all the important details about your furniture, such as its dimensions, condition, and materials.
  • Be patient: It may take some time to sell your furniture, so don't get discouraged if it doesn't sell right away.

By following these tips, you can increase your chances of selling your secondhand furniture quickly and for a good price.

Clean and repair: Make sure your furniture is clean and in good condition before selling it.

Before you list your furniture for sale, it's important to clean it thoroughly and make any necessary repairs. This will make your furniture more appealing to potential buyers and help you get a better price for it.

Here are some tips for cleaning and repairing your furniture:

  • Clean all surfaces: Use a mild soap and water solution to clean all surfaces of your furniture, including the upholstery, wood, and metal. Be sure to rinse the furniture thoroughly and dry it completely before storing or selling it.
  • Repair any damage: If your furniture has any damage, such as scratches, dents, or tears, repair it before selling it. This will make your furniture look more appealing and increase its value.
  • Tighten any loose screws or bolts: Over time, the screws and bolts on your furniture can become loose. Tighten any loose hardware before selling your furniture to ensure that it is sturdy and safe.
  • Reupholster or refinish your furniture (optional): If your furniture is looking a bit dated, you can reupholster or refinish it to give it a fresh new look. This can be a great way to increase the value of your furniture and make it more appealing to potential buyers.

By following these tips, you can clean and repair your furniture to make it look its best and get the best possible price for it.

Once your furniture is clean and in good condition, you can start preparing it for sale. This includes taking good photos, writing a detailed description, and pricing it correctly.

Price it right: Research similar items online to get an idea of what your furniture is worth.

Once you have cleaned and repaired your furniture, it's time to price it. The best way to do this is to research similar items online to get an idea of what they are selling for.

  • Check eBay and Craigslist: eBay and Craigslist are two of the most popular online marketplaces for used furniture. You can search for similar items to yours and see what they are selling for.
  • Use a furniture pricing guide: There are a number of online furniture pricing guides available, such as the NADAguides Furniture Pricing Guide. These guides can give you an estimate of the value of your furniture based on its age, condition, and style.
  • Consider your local market: The price of furniture can vary depending on your location. For example, furniture tends to be more expensive in large cities than in small towns. Keep this in mind when pricing your furniture.
  • Set a competitive price: When pricing your furniture, it's important to set a competitive price. If you price your furniture too high, it will be difficult to sell. However, if you price it too low, you will not get the best possible price for it.

By following these tips, you can price your furniture right and increase your chances of selling it quickly and for a good price.

Take good photos: Clear, well-lit photos will make your furniture more appealing to potential buyers.

The photos you use in your listing are one of the most important factors in selling your furniture online. Clear, well-lit photos will make your furniture look more appealing and increase your chances of selling it quickly and for a good price.

Here are some tips for taking good photos of your furniture:

  • Use natural light: If possible, take your photos in natural light. This will give your photos a brighter, more inviting look.
  • Take multiple photos: Take several photos of your furniture from different angles. This will give potential buyers a better idea of what your furniture looks like.
  • Stage your furniture: Before you take your photos, stage your furniture to make it look its best. This means arranging your furniture in a way that is both attractive and functional.
  • Edit your photos: Once you have taken your photos, you can edit them to improve their quality. This includes cropping the photos, adjusting the brightness and contrast, and removing any unwanted objects.

By following these tips, you can take good photos of your furniture that will make it more appealing to potential buyers.

Once you have taken good photos of your furniture, you can start writing a detailed description. This description should include all the important details about your furniture, such as its dimensions, condition, and materials.

Write a detailed description: Include all the important details about your furniture, such as its dimensions, condition, and materials.

Once you have taken good photos of your furniture, it's time to write a detailed description. This description should include all the important details about your furniture, such as its dimensions, condition, and materials.

  • Dimensions: Be sure to include the dimensions of your furniture in your description. This will help potential buyers to determine if the furniture will fit in their space.
  • Condition: Describe the condition of your furniture in detail. Be honest about any damage or wear and tear. This will help potential buyers to make an informed decision about whether or not to purchase your furniture.
  • Materials: List the materials that your furniture is made of. This will help potential buyers to determine if the furniture is made of high-quality materials.
  • Other important details: Include any other important details about your furniture in your description. This could include information about the furniture's history, provenance, or any special features.

By including all of these important details in your description, you can help potential buyers to make an informed decision about whether or not to purchase your furniture.

Be patient: It may take some time to sell your furniture, so don't get discouraged if it doesn't sell right away.

Once you have listed your furniture for sale, it's important to be patient. It may take some time to sell your furniture, especially if you are asking for a high price. Don't get discouraged if your furniture doesn't sell right away. Just keep promoting your listing and be patient.

Here are some tips for being patient while selling your furniture:

  • Set realistic expectations: Don't expect your furniture to sell overnight. It may take some time to find the right buyer. Set realistic expectations and be patient.
  • Promote your listing: Once you have listed your furniture for sale, promote it on social media and other online platforms. This will help to get your listing seen by more potential buyers.
  • Be flexible: Be willing to negotiate on the price of your furniture if you are not getting any offers. You may also want to consider offering delivery or other incentives to potential buyers.
  • Don't give up: If you don't sell your furniture right away, don't give up. Just keep promoting your listing and be patient. Eventually, you will find the right buyer for your furniture.

By following these tips, you can be patient while selling your furniture and increase your chances of getting a good price for it.
