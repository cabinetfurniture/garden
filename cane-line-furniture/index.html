FAQ

Have questions about garden furniture? We've got answers!

Question 1: What is the best material for garden furniture?
Answer: The best material for garden furniture depends on your needs and preferences. Teak wood is a popular choice for its durability and resistance to rot and decay. Synthetic materials like Cane-line Weave® are also great options as they are weather-resistant and low-maintenance. Aluminum is another durable and lightweight option.

Question 2: How can I protect my garden furniture from the elements?
Answer: To protect your garden furniture from the elements, consider using weather-resistant covers or storing it in a dry place during harsh weather. Regular cleaning and maintenance can also help extend the lifespan of your furniture.

Question 3: What are some comfortable seating options for my garden?
Answer: Look for lounge chairs, sofas, and armchairs with thick cushions and ergonomic designs. Adjustable furniture pieces allow you to customize your comfort level. Consider adding throw pillows and blankets for extra comfort and style.

Question 4: How can I add color and personality to my garden space with furniture?
Answer: Choose garden furniture in vibrant colors or with unique patterns to create a lively atmosphere. Mix and match different styles and textures to add visual interest. Incorporate colorful cushions, throws, and outdoor rugs to enhance the overall look.

Question 5: What are some space-saving garden furniture options?
Answer: Consider modular furniture pieces that can be easily reconfigured to suit your needs. Folding chairs and tables are great for small spaces as they can be stored away when not in use. Wall-mounted furniture and vertical planters can also help save space and create a cozy ambiance.

Question 6: How can I create a cohesive look with my garden furniture?
Answer: Choose furniture pieces from the same collection or with similar design elements to create a cohesive look. Stick to a neutral color palette and add pops of color with cushions, throws, and accessories. Consider the overall style of your garden and choose furniture that complements it.
